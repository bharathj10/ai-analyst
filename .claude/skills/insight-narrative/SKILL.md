# Insight narrative

## When to use

Whenever analysis output (tables, numbers, charts) needs to be communicated to a human reader, especially executives.

## The BLUF principle

**Bottom Line Up Front.** The first sentence of any output answers the question. Evidence comes after.

❌ "Over the past quarter, we examined member contribution patterns across 12 cohorts to understand engagement..."
✅ "Member contributions grew 8.2% in Q3, driven entirely by the 35-44 age cohort."

## Structure for any insight

Each finding follows this pattern:
1. **The claim** (one sentence, with the number)
2. **The evidence** (what shows it — chart or table reference)
3. **The implication** (why it matters — one sentence)

Example:
> Member contributions grew 8.2% in Q3 ($1.2M increase, n=12,400). The growth was concentrated in the 35-44 age cohort (+18.4%), while 25-34 stayed flat. *This suggests our recent salary-sacrifice campaign reached the target segment effectively.*

## Voice and phrasing

### Use
- Specific numbers with units: "$1.2M", "8.2%", "12,400 members"
- Comparisons with a clear baseline: "up 8% vs Q2", "vs the 5-year average of 6.1%"
- Active verbs: "grew", "fell", "shifted", "concentrated"
- Australian English: analyse, organisation, behaviour, prioritise, customise

### Avoid
- Hedging adverbs: "significantly", "substantially", "considerably" — let numbers speak
- Vague descriptors: "strong growth", "marked decline" — quantify
- Passive voice: "was observed to be" → "is"
- Filler: "It is worth noting that" → just state the thing

## Caveats section

Always include. Cover at minimum:
- Time period limits ("Data covers Jan-Sep 2025 only")
- Sample size for any segment with n < 100
- Known data quality issues
- Anything the analyst flagged

## Length guidance

| Audience | Format | Length |
|---|---|---|
| C-suite | Email + 3 bullets | ≤ 100 words |
| Senior leaders | One-pager | 300-500 words |
| Working team | Full report | 1000-2000 words |
| Technical peers | Notebook + appendix | Whatever fits |

Default to the shorter version unless asked otherwise. People read shorter.
